AVRO

Avro Life Sciences is a biotechnology startup based out of the Velocity Science incubator in Waterloo, Ontario, creating transdermal patches to deliver antihistamines to both ends of the age spectrum (children and seniors). As CTO, I am - Leading R&D efforts on our first patch prototypes, requiring extensive research into pharmacokinetics, skin chemistry, strateum corneum manipulation, and drug chemistry - Simultaneously developing a series of lab plans, starting with a Franz Diffusion cell proof-of-concept test and progressing to experiments with varied drug matrix formulations - Gained experience working with FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy), HPLC (High Performance Liquid Chromatography) and SEM (Scanning Electron Microscope) analytical techniques.

  • Employer: University of Toronto
  • Date: Summers 2013-2015
  • Skills developed: Molecular Biology Thoery, Basic Circuity, Soldering, Cell Culture, qPCR, Western Blotting, Clonogenics, Bioprinting, Biochemistry

TRACER & Gelatin Pen

Assisted in the development of TRACER (Tissue Roll for Analysis of Cellular Environment Response), a novel in vitro tumour model allowing for the recapitulation of many spatial aspects of the in vivo tumour microenvironment. Played a significant role in designing and prototyping the first custom-built gelatin dispensing "pen", allowing the user to deposit temporary gelatin barriers into a TRACER scaffold to define domain boundaries between different cell types, to allow for better replication of accurate in vivo tumour microenvironments
